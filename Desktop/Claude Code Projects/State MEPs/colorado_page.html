About Us

Partners for manufacturing success

Colorado roots, national impact. We are Manufacturer’s Edge, Colorado’s official MEP Center and dedicated partner to manufacturers across our state and beyond.

to bottom
Our Story

More than manufacturing

As Colorado’s official Manufacturing Extension Partnership (MEP) Center, we provide onsite support, strategy, planning, training, and technical assistance to manufacturers of all sizes. Colorado’s unique manufacturing landscape has given us experience across industries. Our clients drive technology development, creativity, and new products, and we work closely with them to make sure their work reaches its intended audience. Local insights, diverse experience, and true dedication to providing manufacturers support – use the support we provide to empower your decisions as you continue to grow and improve the manufacturing landscape.

Part of the MEP National Network

The MEP National Network is a unique public-private partnership that delivers comprehensive, proven solutions to U.S. manufacturers, fueling growth and advancing U.S. manufacturing. Focused on helping manufacturers generate business results and thrive in today’s technology-driven economy, the MEP National Network comprises the National Institute of Standards and Technology’s Manufacturing Extension Partnership (NIST MEP), 51 MEP Centers located in all 50 states and Puerto Rico, and its 1,300 trusted advisors and experts at nearly 600 MEP service locations, providing any U.S. manufacturer with access to resources they need to succeed.

Vice President & COO

Favorite project helping a manufacturer:

My favorite projects with manufacturers are the ones where we have a chance to get to know the company, the business, and the people and are able to bring resources to needs seen and unseen. The manufacturer that started because they loved making things and now, due to their own success, have found themselves making money and having to run a business that needs to add employees, scale operations, and deal with the every-day challenges, but are still using spreadsheets to manage data and want to keep their culture and their people – these are my favorite clients.

What do you love about your role?

Everything. After having worked in the private sector, federal and state government, and at a university, I can honestly say that serving as the COO for Manufacturer’s Edge is the most fun I have ever had at work. I love that in my role I get to help lead a non-profit with a mission that runs like a business with a team of smart, talented, and interesting individuals with diverse workstyles and personalities that are each valued for the unique perspectives and solutions they bring to our work every day.
